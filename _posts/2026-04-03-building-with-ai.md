AI isn't replacing developers. It's replacing developers who don't use AI.

## The Shift Has Happened

The debate is over. AI tools have crossed the threshold from "interesting experiment" to "daily necessity." The question is no longer whether to use them, but how to use them well.

## What Actually Changed

- **Prototyping** is 10x faster
- **Boilerplate** is nearly free
- **Context switching** is reduced — you can stay in flow longer
- **The ceiling** has risen — individuals can now do what used to require teams

## What Hasn't Changed

- Taste and judgment
- Understanding what to build
- Knowing when something is wrong
- Communication with other humans

The bottleneck has shifted from *can I build this* to *should I build this*.

## The New Skill

Learning to work with AI effectively is becoming as fundamental as learning to type. It's not magic — it's a tool. And like any tool, you have to learn to use it properly.

The builders who thrive are those who use AI to amplify their judgment, not replace it.
