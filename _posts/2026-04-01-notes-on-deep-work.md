Cal Newport defined deep work as *professional activity performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that pushes your cognitive capabilities to their limit.*

## Why It Matters

The ability to do deep work is becoming increasingly rare and increasingly valuable. Most knowledge work is increasingly shallow — meetings, emails, Slack messages. The person who can carve out time for real concentration has a competitive advantage.

## My System

**Morning blocks** — The first 2-3 hours of the day are protected. No meetings, no email, no notifications.

**Single-tasking** — One task at a time. The browser has one tab. The phone is face-down.

**Shutdown rituals** — A deliberate end to the workday. Every open loop is captured. When I close the laptop, work is done.

## The Paradox

The better you get at deep work, the more you can accomplish in less time. This creates space for rest, which makes the next deep work session better.

It's a virtuous cycle. But it requires defending your time aggressively.

> "A deep life is a good life."
