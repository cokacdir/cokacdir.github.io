Simplicity is not the absence of complexity. It is the mastery of it.

## Less, But Better

The German designer Dieter Rams captured this with his famous principle: *weniger, aber besser* — less, but better. Every decision should have a reason. Every element should earn its place.

## The Iceberg Principle

Great design hides its complexity beneath the surface. The user sees only what they need to see. The rest is handled silently, invisibly, without friction.

> "Perfection is achieved, not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

## How to Get There

1. **Start with the problem**, not the solution
2. **Remove before you add** — ask what can be eliminated
3. **Test with real people** — what's obvious to you isn't obvious to others
4. **Iterate** — simplicity is never first draft

The pursuit of simplicity is a practice, not a destination.
