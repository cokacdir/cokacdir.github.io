Something is shifting in the Korean startup ecosystem. It feels different this time.

## What's Changed

The old playbook was: copy a successful Western product, localize it, dominate the Korean market. That worked well for a generation.

Now the playbook is changing. Korean companies are building for the world from day one. The language barrier, once a ceiling, is becoming a wall that competitors can't easily cross.

## AI as the Equalizer

AI tools have dramatically leveled the playing field for non-English speakers. The translation gap is closing. Korean founders can now ship to global markets faster than ever.

## What to Watch

- **B2B SaaS** — Long underrepresented, now seeing serious investment
- **AI-native tools** — Several quietly impressive companies building in this space
- **Creator economy** — Korea's cultural export momentum is creating unique opportunities

## The Vibe

There's an energy here that I haven't felt since the early days of mobile. The timing feels right. The talent is here. The capital is slowly following.

It's going to be an interesting few years.
